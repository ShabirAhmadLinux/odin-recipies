Short description :

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish that has found a warm welcome in many parts of the world. It's traditionally made by stacking spiced meat (usually lamb, chicken, beef, or turkey) on a vertical rotisserie, which slowly turns and cooks the meat. As the outer layer of the meat roasts to perfection, it's shaved off in thin slices and typically served in a flatbread or pita. Accompaniments can include a variety of fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions, along with creamy sauces such as tahini, garlic sauce, or hummus.

What makes shawarma a delightful culinary experience is the intricate blend of spices used to marinate the meat, often including garlic, turmeric, cardamom, and more. The slow-cooking process allows the flavors to meld together beautifully, resulting in a succulent and flavorful dish.
